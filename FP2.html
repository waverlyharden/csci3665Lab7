Choropleth A choropleth is a map in which different regions are shaded to highlight a particular attribute. Because there is so much data pertaining to location in the Furbish dataset, it might be beneficial to have a choropleth that shows where most of the discoveries were made. The colors could indicate how many discoveries were made in specific places. This could also be tailored to only show a single species of plant and where it was discovered across the U.S.



Bar Chart Because there were so many samples that were collected in various regions, a bar chart that shows the frequencies that each species of plant was found would be really useful in visualizing how often species were found.



Heatmap The Furbish dataset has a lot of information for each individual species such as leaf size and color. It might be useful to use a heatmap in order to show the correlation between an attribute like leaf size to each individual species. This visualization could also be used for many of the attributes besides just leaf size or color, so there could be a potential dropdown menu to change the attribute used.



Histogram We could also use a histogram to bin the species based on the quality of their attributes. This would show us a range of the values. As you changed the range, there would be more and more bins. This would also be effective if it showed the names of the species included in each bin with a mouseover or tooltip. Like the Heatmap, the attributes can be easily changed so a dropdown menu might be useful here.



Scatterplot For the scatterplot, it might be useful to use a time series graph to show how many species were discovered at a certain time. Each specie could be represented as a circle and when you mouseover, you could see the specie name and location. The time series could also use a zoom or drag so you only look at part of the timeline.
