Final Project

Choropleth Prototype: Our Choropleth Prototype was going to be used to show where most of Kate Furbish's discoveries were made. In showing the prototype to the class, many people thought that the choropleth would work best if we figure out how to use the corrdinates of the locations that were visited because it might work better for coding the choropleth. Additionally, people thought that our Choropleth should show the overall accumulation of locations visited because then you can see where all of the discoveries were made instead of just a few in time.

Scatterplot Prototype: For our Scatterplot Prototype we were planning on addressing a series of discoveries over time. The majority of the feedback we received for this visualization was to clarift what was actually going to be shown on the scatterplot. This means that for a given time on the x-axis, what was shown. Is it a single circle or multiple? People reccomended that each point be an individual discovery in the scatterplot. Also people said that for this visualization, what might be most effective is if we have a dropdown menu with each specie or year to show the relationship that time has on Furbish's discovery trends. People also recommended that we possibly include a brush or zoom, so that we can see changes within specific years since the range of years is so large.

Histogram Prototype: For the Histogram Prototype we were planning on showing how often Kate Furbish was able to find species based on their common name over time. The feedback that we received for this project suggested that we add more functionality to the visualization. Right now, we have buttons for showing specific years in the dataset. Some people thought that the Histogram was too similar to the scatterplot and recommended showing this information as a multi-line graph for each specie family or common name over time. This might make understanding the information easier as well and would give better context over time.

Bar Graph Prototype: For the Bar Graph Prototype we were planning on showing how often Kate Furbish was able to find species. For this visualization, we also thought of creating a stacked bar chart that could potentially show the incorrect vs. correct classifications of each species.Right now, we have buttons for sorting and for showing every five years. For this visualization, people recommended that we include a dropdown menu of each specie family. This would show the bars for each specie within the family which would be useful in seeing which species are more commonly found within a family. This might be necessary because people also thought that showing all of the species on a single graph would be too hard to see.
